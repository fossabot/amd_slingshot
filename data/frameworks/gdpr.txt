General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
Regulation (EU) 2016/679
Issued by: European Union
Effective: May 25, 2018

Overview
The General Data Protection Regulation establishes rules for the protection of natural persons with regard to the processing of personal data and the free movement of such data.

Article 5 – Principles of Data Processing
Personal data must be:
1. Processed lawfully, fairly, and transparently.
2. Collected for specified, explicit, and legitimate purposes.
3. Adequate, relevant, and limited to what is necessary (data minimization).
4. Accurate and kept up to date.
5. Stored only for as long as necessary.
6. Processed securely using appropriate technical and organizational measures.

Article 6 – Lawfulness of Processing
Processing of personal data is lawful only if at least one of the following applies:
• Consent of the data subject
• Performance of a contract
• Legal obligation
• Protection of vital interests
• Public interest or official authority
• Legitimate interests pursued by the controller

Article 25 – Data Protection by Design and by Default
Organizations must implement technical and organizational measures designed to implement data protection principles effectively. Systems processing personal data should ensure only the minimum necessary data is processed.

Article 32 – Security of Processing
Organizations must implement appropriate security measures including:
• Encryption or pseudonymization of personal data
• Ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability
• Ability to restore availability of data after incidents
• Regular testing and evaluation of security controls

AI Relevance
AI systems processing personal data must ensure:
• Lawful data collection
• Transparency of automated decision making
• Data minimization
• Secure storage and processing
• Human oversight where necessary
